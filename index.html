Why Scouting

What your child gains from every Friday evening

Scouting builds things that don't appear on a school report: the ability to navigate when lost, to stay calm in an emergency, to lead a team, and to keep going when it gets hard. These aren't abstract values — they're practised skills, earned through real outdoor experiences.

🧭 Navigation & orienteering
🩹 First aid & safety
🤝 Leadership & teamwork
🏕 Outdoor & survival skills
What we get up to

From the troop's recent history

Real stories from real Fridays, camps, and community days — updated as we go.

Scouts Dec 2022

A slice of kindness — Streetkids Christmas Feast

When Henley Soup Souls planned a Christmas feast for 60 underprivileged children, 1st Henley Scouts stepped up without hesitation. Our scouts helped serve, organise, and bring joy to children who needed it most.

Read more →
Scouts Oct 2021

Second annual Thenius Hook Potjie Competition

An early start, a walk to the bowling club, and a morning of pioneering and potjie cooking. Scouts led cubs through the challenge — and the results were impressive (and edible).

Read more →
Scouts Mar 2022

SSAM Magaliesberg — 40km through mountains, rivers and forest

The Magaliesberg mountains tested everything. River crossings, dense forest, brutal terrain, and brilliant weather on day two. This is what adventure looks like.

Read more →
Scouts Apr 2019

Lion's Club Treehouse — scouts pioneer for community

The troop spent two days building a treehouse-of-sorts for the Lion's Club fundraiser, staying up in the structure until R5,000 was raised for community upliftment.

Read more →
Scouts Feb 2020

Firefighters scoutcraft badge day at Randvaal Fire Station

Over 20 scouts spent a day at Randvaal Fire learning real firefighting techniques — extinguishers, hose handling, and what to do in a fire emergency. Badge earned.

All branches Jan 2022

AGM 2022 — renewing our commitment after two tough Covid years

Our 2022 AGM was a moment to reflect, say thank you, and recommit. Covid changed everything — but the troop came back stronger.

About the award

What is the Springbok award?

The Springbok award is the pinnacle of achievement in SCOUTS South Africa — the equivalent of the Eagle Scout in the USA or the Queen's Scout in the UK. It is awarded to scouts who demonstrate outstanding character, skill, service, and leadership over years of dedicated scouting.

To earn the Springbok, a scout must complete progressively demanding advancement stages — Discoverer, Explorer, Adventurer, Pioneer — before undertaking a substantial community or conservation project as the capstone.

It is not a badge handed out lightly. Every Springbok on this page is a testament to years of effort, perseverance, and genuine contribution.

Requirements include

  • Completion of Discoverer, Explorer, Adventurer & Pioneer ranks
  • A minimum number of night camping experiences
  • Demonstrated leadership within the troop
  • First aid certification to the required level
  • A significant community service or conservation project
  • A formal review by a SCOUTS SA assessment board

The journey

The road to Springbok

The Springbok is the final stage of a progressive advancement pathway that can take years to complete. Each rank builds on the last.

Discoverer
First advancement rank. Basic outdoor skills, badge work, and understanding of the Scout Promise and Law.
Explorer
Navigation, camping, community service, first aid, and demonstrated patrol leadership.
Adventurer
Multi-day expeditions, advanced scoutcraft, leadership of junior scouts, and expanded service contribution.
Pioneer
Full leadership responsibilities within the troop, significant service project planning, and advanced skills demonstration.
🏅 Springbok
The capstone. A community or conservation project of real scope and impact, assessed by a SCOUTS SA board.
